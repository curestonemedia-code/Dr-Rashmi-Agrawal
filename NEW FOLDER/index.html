06  /  Questions

Everything patients ask.

The most common concerns we hear in the consultation room, answered honestly. If yours isn't here, message us directly.

Ask on WhatsApp
Is laser surgery really painless?
The procedure itself is done under anaesthesia, so you feel nothing during. Post-operatively, because there are no cuts or incisions, discomfort is typically mild — most patients are off pain medication within 24–48 hours. Compared to open or PCNL surgery, the pain profile is dramatically lower.
What does "fluoroscopy-free" actually mean, and why should I care?
Most stone surgeries worldwide still use real-time X-ray (fluoroscopy) to guide instruments. That means repeated radiation exposure for you, the surgeon, and OT staff — session after session. My protocol uses high-resolution ultrasound and direct endoscopic vision instead, delivering zero radiation with equal or better precision. Particularly important for young patients, women of reproductive age, and anyone who may need repeat procedures.
How long until I can go back to work?
For RIRS/laser stone surgery, most patients resume desk work within 48–72 hours and full physical activity within a week. For HoLEP (prostate surgery), light activity resumes in 3–5 days, heavy exertion after 2 weeks. Exact timeline depends on your case — we'll give you a specific plan at discharge.
Will my health insurance cover the surgery?
Yes, in the vast majority of cases. We're empanelled with most major Indian insurers including Star Health, HDFC Ergo, ICICI Lombard, Bajaj Allianz, CGHS and ECHS. Our team handles cashless pre-authorisation directly — send us your policy details along with reports on WhatsApp and we'll confirm coverage before your appointment.
What if the stone doesn't fully clear in one session?
Our single-session clearance rate is 98%+ even for complex and staghorn stones. For the rare case where residual fragments remain (usually in very large or multi-locational stones), a short second session is planned and typically included under the same treatment package — no additional surprise cost.
Can I consult online before travelling to Gurugram?
Absolutely. Many of our patients travel from outside Delhi NCR. Send your reports, CT/ultrasound scans, and prior prescriptions on WhatsApp for a written preliminary opinion within 2 hours. If surgery is indicated, we coordinate travel, stay, and discharge timing so you can be in-and-out within 48 hours.
What are the typical costs?
Transparent pricing based on the procedure and complexity. Laser stone removal (RIRS) starts from ₹85,000; Mini-PCNL from ₹1,20,000; HoLEP from ₹1,50,000. Final cost depends on stone burden, hospital stay, and any implants (DJ stent, etc.). We provide a written estimate before admission — no hidden charges. Most costs are fully covered under cashless insurance.
What happens after discharge? Is there follow-up?
Yes — follow-up is part of the treatment, not an afterthought. You'll have a scheduled review at 2 weeks for DJ stent removal (if placed), then a 6-week metabolic evaluation to understand why the stone formed and prevent recurrence. WhatsApp access to our team remains open throughout.
